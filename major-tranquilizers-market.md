<p><p>The major tranquilizers market can be categorized into three generations. First-generation tranquilizers, also known as typical antipsychotics, primarily focus on dopamine antagonism and include drugs like haloperidol. Second-generation, or atypical antipsychotics, offer a broader mechanism of action and include medications like risperidone and olanzapine, targeting both dopamine and serotonin receptors for improved efficacy and fewer side effects. Third-generation antipsychotics, such as aripiprazole, introduce partial agonism, providing a more balanced approach to managing symptoms with potentially enhanced tolerability and reduced risks.</p></p>
