<p><p>Trackpad buttons are categorized into two main market types: Single Touch and Multi-point Touch. Single Touch trackpads respond to one finger input, making them ideal for basic navigation tasks. In contrast, Multi-point Touch trackpads allow multiple fingers to interact simultaneously, enabling advanced gestures like pinch-to-zoom and swipe actions. The demand for Multi-point Touch is rising, driven by consumer preferences for more intuitive controls and enhanced functionality in devices like laptops, tablets, and smartphones.</p></p>
