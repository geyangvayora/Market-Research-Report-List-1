<p><p>The termite treatment market comprises two primary types: non-chemical and chemical treatment. Non-chemical treatments include physical barriers, heat treatment, and borate applications, focusing on prevention and remediation without harmful substances. In contrast, chemical treatments involve the application of insecticides, either through localized injections or widespread soil treatment, effectively targeting existing infestations. Both methods aim to protect structures from termite damage, with consumer preference often guided by considerations of safety, environmental impact, and effectiveness.</p></p>
