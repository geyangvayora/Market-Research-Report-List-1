<p><p>The apple pectin market is divided into two primary types: dry pectin and liquid pectin. Dry pectin is a powdered form that is convenient for baking and preserves, providing strong gelling and thickening properties when mixed with sugar and fruit. Liquid pectin, on the other hand, is pre-gelled and typically used for quick jams and jellies, allowing for easier incorporation into recipes. Both types cater to different culinary applications, impacting their usage in food production and home cooking.</p></p>
