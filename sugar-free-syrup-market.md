<p><p>The sugar-free syrup market primarily consists of three types: erythritol, sucralose, and other sugar substitutes. Erythritol is a sugar alcohol known for its low-calorie content and minimal impact on blood sugar levels, making it a popular choice for health-conscious consumers. Sucralose is an artificial sweetener that is significantly sweeter than sugar and frequently used in various food products. Other market alternatives include stevia, monk fruit, and various natural and synthetic sweeteners, catering to diverse consumer preferences for sugar alternatives.</p></p>
