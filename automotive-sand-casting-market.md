<p><p>Automotive sand casting is a manufacturing process used to produce complex metal components, primarily from aluminum and iron, for the automotive industry. Technological advancements, such as the integration of automation and simulation software in the casting process, are also contributing to improved efficiency and reduced production costs. <p><p>The automotive sand casting market encompasses various types of sand used for creating molds in the casting process. Green sand is the most common, made from a mixture of sand, clay, and water, allowing for excellent molding properties. Dry sand is used for precision molds and requires a binder, making it suitable for larger castings. Loam sand, a mixture of sand, clay, and water, is employed in complex shapes and intricate designs. Other types include resin sand and shell sand, each serving specialized applications.</p></p>
